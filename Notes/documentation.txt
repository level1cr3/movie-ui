### ğŸ”¹ Skeletons vs. Spinners

1. **Skeletons (preferred for content-heavy pages)**

   * Used when the page has a lot of structured content (cards, lists, tables, forms).
   * Makes the app *feel faster* because users see the layout instantly, and then data "fills in".
   * Example: Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube â†’ all use skeletons for feeds or videos.

   ğŸ‘‰ Usually built with libraries like [shadcn/ui skeleton](https://ui.shadcn.com/docs/components/skeleton) or custom components.

2. **Spinners (used for small or global states)**

   * For quick, *short* loading states (e.g., submitting a form, fetching a small dataset).
   * Often used as an **overlay spinner** for "blocking" actions.
   * Example: When you click "Save" on a form, a spinner in the button or a small overlay is enough.

   ğŸ‘‰ Global loading spinners are rare for *full pages* nowadays (feels slower, less polished).

---

### ğŸ”¹ Common Industry Approach

* **Skeletons for page-level content** â†’ improves perceived performance.
* **Button/inline spinners for actions** â†’ e.g., inside a submit button.
* **Global loader/spinner overlay** â†’ reserved for rare cases like authentication check, route transition (initial app load).
